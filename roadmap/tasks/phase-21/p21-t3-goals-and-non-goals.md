# P21-T3 - Goals And Non-Goals

## Phase

[Phase 21 - Ralph Product Source of Truth](../../phases/phase-21-ralph-product-source-of-truth.md)

## Objective

Convert the PRD's generic goal and non-goal sections into explicit Ralph product priorities and boundaries.

## Deliverables

- Define must-have goals for Ralph intake, workspace bootstrap, controlled runs, evidence capture, delivery, and recovery.
- Define should-have goals for portfolio coordination, blueprint reuse, learning feedback, and maintenance workflows.
- Define non-goals that prevent drift into a general IDE, generic terminal multiplexer, cloud orchestration service, unmanaged autonomous agent, or replacement for Claude Code.
- Document the intended role of editor, diff, terminal, settings, and history as operator support tools.
- Add priority levels that make Ralph's primary project lifecycle outrank generic shell polish.
- Identify which existing capabilities are retained for support rather than expanded as primary product bets.

## Dependencies

- Product definition and users/jobs should be clear enough to prioritize goals.
- Existing roadmap phases 1-20 are available for historical context.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Goals in `PRD.md` map to Ralph user outcomes and observable product behavior.
- Non-goals explicitly rule out generic wrapper expansion when it conflicts with Ralph-first workflows.
- The goals can be used to decide whether future feature requests belong in the primary roadmap.
- No placeholder goal or non-goal language remains in the edited sections.
